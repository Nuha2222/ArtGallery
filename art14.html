Ballet at the Paris Opéra
Hilaire Germain Edgar Degas
1877
Pastel over monotype on cream laid paper
Plate: 35.2 × 70.6 cm (13 7/8 × 27 13/16 in.); Sheet: 35.9 × 71.9 cm (14 3/16 × 28 5/16 in.)

Edgar Degas innovatively combined monotype and pastel techniques in Ballet at the Paris Opéra to capture the fleeting beauty of ballet dancers. Using unconventional perspectives and cropped forms, the painting evokes the spontaneity of a rehearsal, with the dancers' free-flowing hair and the intrusion of orchestra instruments enhancing the immediacy of the scene.
